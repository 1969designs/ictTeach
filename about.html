About Me

Welcome to my new site

My name is Keith George: qualified teacher of History whose growing fascination through the nineties & early-noughts with computers, led to reinvention as a Teacher of ICT.

Born in England my family emmigrated to the North Island of New Zealand when I was 4 before moving to Australia where I completed High School before returning to the UK in 1986. Back in the UK I finished off my education including 3 years in Liverpool & 1 in Leicester for my PGCSE.

I taught ICT for 7 years before the opportunity to become the School's Virtual Learning Manager (VLM) proved too good an opportunity to ignore. I had been building websites for a number of years as a learning platform for the studfents I taught: web pages were my lesson plans with the resources they needed a click away; homework tasks & deadlines were always accessible; & I also hosted activities & links to resources that would give my students the chance to extend & develop their skills & understanding.

As VLM I was responsible for the building & maintaining the School's outward-facing website as well as developing the design & promoting the use of the Microsoft Sharepoint Virtual Learning Environment the school had recently invested in.

In 2010 I left my role as VLM to be a Carer which I did upto September 2018.

I am now back looking for work as a Teacher of ICT & I hope this site has given you more of an idea about who I am & some of the things I can do.

If you have any questions please do not hesitate to contact me.

About this Site

Credits: the People & Sites that Helped

The main purposes of this website are:

  • as a means to brush up on my webdesign and HTML & CSS coding skills.
  • as a platform to practice, store & display new & more advanced coding & programing work.
  • as an active library of lessons & resources to use with my students.

Currently this site is still under construction & going through the main initial design phase, which evolves as I'm introduced to new web technologies and practices... & while I make the tricky choice of site name.

This site is being designed to be Responsive to the different ways in which people view the Internet & Accessible, to ensure that as many people as possible may make use of it.

What started out as a quick recap of HTML & CSS knowledge & skills soon became a bigger dive into the new technologies of HTML5 & CSS3.
